What are the Symptoms of Type 2 Diabetes?

Frequent Urination & Increased Thirst

Frequent urination, especially at night, and increased thirst are common symptoms of diabetes. High glucose levels cause the kidneys to work harder to remove excess sugar, resulting in increased urine production. If you’re experiencing these symptoms, it’s crucial to get checked for diabetes.

Know Your Diet

Stay hydrated with water and avoid sugary drinks. Focus on a balanced diet with plenty of vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.

Consistent Fatigue

Persistent feelings of fatigue and weakness could signal diabetes. When your body can’t effectively use glucose for energy, you may feel tired constantly. Don’t ignore ongoing fatigue; it could be related to blood sugar issues.

Know Your Diet

Incorporate complex carbohydrates like brown rice and whole grains, and ensure you're eating enough fiber-rich foods such as fruits and vegetables.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Unintended weight loss, even with an increased appetite, can be a sign of diabetes. When the body can’t use glucose, it starts breaking down fat and muscle for energy. If you’re losing weight without trying, diabetes might be a cause.

Know Your Diet

Ensure you're consuming enough calories from healthy sources. Incorporate nuts, avocados, and lean meats into your meals for adequate energy.

Vision Issues

Blurred vision can result from high blood sugar damaging blood vessels in the eyes. If you experience this symptom, seek medical advice as it may indicate uncontrolled diabetes.

Know Your Diet

Include foods rich in antioxidants like leafy greens and berries to support eye health, and manage blood sugar levels.

Slow Healing Wounds

High blood sugar can slow blood flow and collagen production, delaying wound healing. If cuts or bruises are slow to heal, check your blood sugar levels.

Know Your Diet

Consume vitamin C-rich foods like oranges and bell peppers to help with collagen production and wound healing.

Nerve Issues in Extremities

Numbness or tingling in hands or feet can indicate nerve damage from high blood sugar. Don't ignore this symptom, as untreated diabetes can lead to complications.

Know Your Diet

Incorporate foods high in omega-3 fatty acids like fish and walnuts to support nerve health.
